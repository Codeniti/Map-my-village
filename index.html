Lines of latitude and longitude are used to locate places on earth by using the geographic coordinate system. Latitude is the angle from a point on the earth's surface and the equatorial plane measured from the center of the sphere. Longitude is the angle east or west of a reference meridian between the two geographical poles to another meridian that passes through an arbitrary point. They are used to locate places on earth by telling you where something is vertically and horizontally on the earth.
